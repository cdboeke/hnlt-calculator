Consider all sources of income including but not limited to: rental income, jobs, gigs, dividends, commissions, money you currently get from family members etc.
Consider your current living situation, including whether you rent, own (outright or paying it off), or live with others.
Consider all student, medical, and consumer debt that you currently have. Do not include mortgage debt, which is accounted for in the housing category.
Consider wealth that you currently have access to because of family or other relationships, as well as what you might inherit or owe when family members pass away. Negative family wealth means you don’t inherit anything of value and have responsibility to pay expenses that come up when a family member dies, like funeral, casket, legal fees, clergy, etc. Wealth you have access to or inherit includes: money, gifts, stocks, land and property, trusts, cars, etc..
Consider your current status. We acknowledge that not all advanced degrees are created equally—some have higher earning potential and social capital than others. We’ve tried to account for this, and yet, encourage you to think about your degree(s) in terms of social hierarchy (e.g. MDs and JDs often have higher earning potential than MFAs).
Consider the people who rely on you for financial support, including children, family members or others.
Consider retirement funds (401k, IRA, SEP, etc), investments in stocks, bonds, and mutual funds, as well as the money that is in your savings account.
